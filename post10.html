Layer 1 Blockchains

  • Ethereum (ETH): The original smart contract giant. While it has faced scalability challenges, it boasts a massive ecosystem of dApps, DeFi protocols, and NFT projects.
  • Solana (SOL): A high-performance blockchain prioritizing blistering speed and low transaction fees. It has rapidly gained traction in the NFT and gaming sectors.
  • Avalanche (AVAX): Emphasizes flexibility with its subnet architecture. Projects can build their own customized blockchains within the Avalanche ecosystem, optimizing for their specific use cases.
  • Sui: A highly anticipated Layer 1 designed for ultra-low latency and horizontal scalability. Sui is being developed by Mysten Labs, formed by ex-Meta (Facebook) engineers and promises groundbreaking innovations.

Layer 2 Scaling Solutions (Built on Ethereum)

  • Base (L2): Coinbase's newly launched Ethereum Layer 2 built on the Optimism stack. It aims to onboard millions of new users into the web3 ecosystem with low fees and a simplified user experience.
  • Optimism (OP): A leading Layer 2 solution employing Optimistic Rollups. By processing transactions off the main Ethereum chain, it offers faster speeds and lower costs.
  • Scroll: An Ethereum L2 focusing on ZK-Rollups (zero-knowledge proofs). ZKPs offer not only scalability improvements but also enhanced privacy features.

Other Notable Blockchains

  • Mode: A blockchain optimized for digital asset management, focusing on the custody and issuance of tokenized financial assets (stocks, bonds, commodities).
  • Mantle: A new modular Ethereum L2 from BitDAO, aiming to combine scalability benefits with a focus on being the data availability layer for other rollups.
  • Blast: A high-performance blockchain focused on cross-chain gaming applications and the metaverse. Still in early development.
